Graded \( K \)-theory

For an ungraded Banach algebra \( A \):

\[ k_A = k(\Mod_A)= \text{group completion of }(\Mod_A^{fgp,\cong},\oplus) \]

Example: \( ko = k_{\R} \), \( k_{C(X;\R)}\simeq ko(X) \).

Definition: Connective graded \( K \)-theory

For a super Banach algebra \( A \), we define graded \( K \)-theory as the fiber:

\[ k^{gr}_A := \fib\left(k(\Mod^{graded}_A) \longrightarrow k(\Mod_{A}^{ungraded})\right) \,. \]
  • Spectral refinement of Karoubi \( K \)-theory.

Theorem (A.-Stehouwer)
